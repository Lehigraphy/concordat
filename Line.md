That morning, Ma had sent him out to get some bread, so now he was standing in a line in the freezing air, surely a thousand people in front of him, strange men with dark helmets and transparent shields watching his every move. Pa would be finally coming home today, and then everything would be good again. He had pushed his hands into the pockets of his coat, making the sleeves bunch up around his wrists and elbows. The air smelled weird, like their new shed mixed with fireworks and cooking. Something was crunching underfoot each time the line edged forwards. A shout made him turn his head, just in time to spot something spinning through the air, glinting, warm yellow and orange flickering.

A sound of glass breaking. A flash of heat on his face. More orange and yellow. Shouts. A scream. Someone dragged away, yelling. Something knocking out his breath. Ground. Something hard on his face.

Gradually, the noise died. Someone was moving, gravel crunching underfoot. Not gravel, he saw. Glass. A shadow passed over him, and he suddenly wanted to be very still. Black boots, worn to grey. A voice, and an extended hand. Slowly, he reached out and squeezed his fingers around its. The glove was rough and hard. The voice was not.
